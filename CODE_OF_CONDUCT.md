# Code Of Conduct

This project aims to be a calm, respectful place to collaborate.

## Our Standard

When participating here, please:

- be clear and kind
- assume good intent, then ask when something is unclear
- focus feedback on code, docs, tests, and behavior
- welcome different experience levels and backgrounds
- keep disagreements technical and specific

Please avoid:

- personal attacks or insulting language
- repeated hostile or dismissive behavior
- harassment, intimidation, or unwanted attention
- sharing private information without permission
- disrupting discussion after a maintainer asks you to stop

## Scope

This applies in issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, and other project spaces.

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove comments and contributions that do not fit this
standard. They may also pause or end interactions when needed to keep the project usable
for everyone.

## Reporting

If you run into a conduct problem, please use GitHub's private security or maintainer
contact flow first rather than escalating in public:

- [Security reporting](./SECURITY.md)
- or open a minimal issue asking for a private contact channel

## Notes

This policy is intentionally short. It is based on the same general principles used by
common open source community standards, including Contributor Covenant.
